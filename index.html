01 — About

Who I Am

I am Ataher Ali, a fisheries researcher and M.S. candidate at the University of Chittagong, Bangladesh. My work bridges computational tools and marine science — I develop open-source R packages for fisheries stock assessment and apply ecological modeling to address pressing conservation challenges across South Asian waters.

I am the author of the aLBI R package on CRAN, designed to estimate fish stock status from length-frequency data — a tool that empowers data-limited fisheries management in developing regions. My research spans ocean acidification, seaweed ecology, mangrove dynamics, and length-based stock assessment of commercially important species.

I hold an IELTS score of 7.0 and am actively seeking PhD opportunities and international research collaborations in fisheries science, marine ecology, and aquatic resource management.

03 — R Package

Software Development

aLBI
An open-source R package published on CRAN designed for estimating Length-Based Indicators (LBIs) for fish stock assessment. It provides a straightforward workflow for data-limited fisheries, allowing researchers and managers to derive stock status indicators directly from length-frequency data — especially valuable in resource-limited settings across South and Southeast Asia.
Version 0.1.9  |  Ali, A., & Alam, M. S. (2024)
View on CRAN
Install in R:
install.packages("aLBI")
