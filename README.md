## 🧬 Research Focus

- **Fisheries Stock Assessment:** Developing length-based, data-limited management frameworks and stock assessment tools for tropical fisheries.
- **Marine Ecology:** Investigating ocean warming (OW) and ocean acidification (OA) impacts in the Bay of Bengal and their consequences for fisheries and seafood.
- **Ecological Modeling:** Utilizing R (aLBI, TropFishR), Ecopath, and MaxEnt for ecosystem-based fisheries analysis and species distribution modeling.
- **Seaweed & Mangrove Ecology:** Assessing ecological services, blue carbon potential, and conservation of coastal ecosystems in Bangladesh.
- **Bycatch & Vulnerability Analysis:** Applying Productivity and Susceptibility Analysis (PSA) to gillnet fisheries bycatch assessment.

## 📦 R Package — aLBI (CRAN)

> **Ali, A., & Alam, M. S. (2024).** *aLBI: Estimating Length-Based Indicators for Fish Stock* (v0.1.9). CRAN.
> 🔗 [https://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/aLBI/index.html](https://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/aLBI/index.html)

```r
install.packages("aLBI")
```

A simple, open-source R package for estimating stock status indicators from length-frequency data — designed for data-limited fisheries management in developing regions.
